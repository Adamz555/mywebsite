Blockchain VM, Gas Model & Related Concepts

Virtual Machine (VM) internals

The blockchain VM (e.g., EVM, WASM-based VMs) executes bytecode deterministically. Key aspects: instruction set, gas costing, stack/stack-framed execution, and state transition functions. Developers should understand gas refunds, storage vs memory costs, and how opcodes map to high-level constructs.

Gas model and cost considerations

Gas provides a resource metering mechanism that prevents infinite loops and assigns economic cost to computation and storage. Optimise for low storage writes and predictable loops.

Additional blockchain concepts

  • State rent — models where long-lived storage requires periodic fees.
  • Sharding — parallelising chain state to increase throughput.
  • Layer 2 — rollups and optimistic / zk techniques for scaling.
  • Cross-chain messaging — secure bridge patterns and relayers.
  • Post-quantum readiness — migrating oracles/keys to PQ schemes where needed.

Practical notes

For auditors: focus on gas-exhaustion attack surfaces, reentrancy across external calls, and assumptions about block/timestamp. For designers: plan upgradeability and strong testing across VM edge-cases.

Conclusion: Understanding VM semantics and higher-level protocol trade-offs is crucial when designing secure, efficient smart contracts and protocols.
