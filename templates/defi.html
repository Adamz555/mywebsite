Decentralized Finance (DeFi)

Introduction

DeFi composes smart contracts, tokens, and oracles into permissionless financial primitives: lending, AMMs, derivatives, and protocol-level governance. DeFi is often described as the Next Evolution of World Financial System because it enables composable financial infrastructure without central intermediaries.

Core building blocks

  • Tokens (ERC20 / ERC721 / ERC1155) — representation of assets.
  • Oracles — reliable price feeds and event attestations.
  • AMMs — automated market makers for liquidity and swaps.
  • Lending protocols — overcollateralized loans, interest markets.
  • Governance — token-weighted proposals and timelocks.

Risks & Mitigations

Smart contract bugs, oracle manipulation, protocol-level accounting errors, and economic attacks (flash-loans) are common. Mitigations: multi-oracle sources, on-chain circuit breakers, and strong invariant testing.

Practical PoC highlights

The research PoCs on the Research Files page illustrate vault primitives and settlement flows. For production DeFi, add price-oracle checks, liquidation mechanisms, and insurance/backstop capital.

Conclusion: DeFi's modularity enables rapid innovation — combine engineering rigor with economic analysis to build resilient financial primitives.
